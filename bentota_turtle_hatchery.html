About Bentota Turtle Hatchery

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is a renowned turtle conservation project located in Bentota, a coastal town in Sri Lanka. It is dedicated to protecting and conserving sea turtles, which are vulnerable and endangered species.

Turtle Conservation

The primary mission of the Bentota Turtle Hatchery is to safeguard sea turtles and their eggs. The hatchery collects turtle eggs from nesting sites along the coastline, where they are at risk from human activities and natural predators. The eggs are carefully incubated in a controlled environment until they hatch, significantly increasing the chances of survival for the baby turtles.

Guided Tour

Visitors to the Bentota Turtle Hatchery can embark on guided tours that provide valuable insights into the lives of different turtle species found in Sri Lanka. Knowledgeable guides share fascinating information about the various species, including the Green Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle. You can learn about their behavior, nesting habits, and the challenges they face in their natural habitat.

Hatchling Release

One of the highlights of visiting the hatchery is the opportunity to actively participate in releasing baby turtles into the ocean. Visitors are usually invited to join in this conservation practice by helping the hatchlings make their way to the water. It is a memorable and rewarding experience as you witness the tiny turtles begin their journey into the sea.

Education and Awareness

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery plays a crucial role in raising awareness about sea turtle conservation. They organize educational programs and presentations to educate visitors about the importance of protecting these incredible creatures. By promoting responsible tourism and sustainable practices, the hatchery aims to inspire visitors to become advocates for turtle conservation.

Rehabilitation Center

In addition to protecting turtle eggs, the hatchery often serves as a rehabilitation center for injured or sick turtles. Skilled staff members provide medical care and nursing assistance to help the injured turtles recover. Once they have regained their health, the turtles are released back into their natural habitat.

Souvenir Shop

Many hatcheries have a souvenir shop where visitors can purchase turtle-themed merchandise, such as clothing, artwork, and handicrafts. The proceeds from these sales contribute to supporting the hatchery's conservation efforts.

Bentota Turtle Hatchery

Visiting the Bentota Turtle Hatchery offers a unique opportunity to connect with sea turtles, learn about their conservation needs, and actively contribute to their survival. It is an enriching experience for nature enthusiasts, families, and individuals interested in marine conservation.
